For years, our ABC company has a chain of 50 F&B outlets in Singapore. This year, we will be opening another FOUR outlets. In line with our promote-from-within policy for outlet managers, we will be announcing the newly created positions to the current pool of assistant managers. We expect 10 to 15 of them to apply.

As the new Talent Acquisition Manager who has just completed a module on talent sourcing and acquisition, please help me redesign our selection process for outlet managers.

Our current process:

Minimum requirements for application: five years of company experience, a university degree, and a minimum acceptable job performance rating of 3. Typically, the decision was made by the CEO via a 5-minute unstructured interview (score: 1 to 10).

The following assessments are readily available in the company for other positions, but they were not used in selecting outlet managers.

  • Cognitive ability test (score: 1 to 10)
  • Background check (ok/not ok)
  • Drug test (P/F)

Recently, I came across a Situational Judgment Test (SJT) on managerial judgement (score 1 to 10) from a reputable test developer. If you think this measure will add value to the selection process, I am open to purchasing it for our use. Please send me the name list of the offer receivers once the selection process is completed.

Thank you.
Director HR
